1947 — 2017

Dr. James Kenechukwu Onwubalili

MD FRCP — Senior Consultant Physician and Nephrologist.
A life defined by excellence, humility, sacrifice and an unwavering commitment to education.

Early Life

From Ogidi to Government College Umuahia

James Kenechukwu Onwubalili (known as “JK”) was born on 24 April 1947 in Ogidi, Anambra State, Nigeria. Tragically, he lost his father, Rev. James Onwubalili, when he was just three years old in 1950. This loss made paying JK’s school fees a significant challenge, as his widowed mother’s limited earnings were insufficient.

After completing his primary education at Ebenezer Primary School in Ogidi, JK was admitted to one of Nigeria’s finest male colleges, Government College Umuahia. There, he excelled through hard work and determination. To complete his secondary education, JK received financial assistance from his uncles, occasional gifts from philanthropists and his mother’s modest savings.

His undergraduate studies at the University of Ibadan were interrupted by the Biafran civil war. During the conflict he joined the Biafran army, rising to the rank of captain, and returned to complete his medical education after the war. James was an outstanding student; he won several prizes and qualified MB BS in 1975 with distinction. As the top student in his class, he was awarded the prestigious Victor Ludorum, collecting medals for medicine, obstetrics, child health and pathology.

Medical Career

From London’s Teaching Hospitals to the Royal College

After his MBBS, JK travelled to the UK for postgraduate studies specialising in nephrology. He trained in renal medicine at some of London’s top universities. His first post was as a renal senior house officer (SHO) at Hammersmith Hospital. He subsequently worked as a renal SHO at Northwick Park Hospital, gained the Membership of the Royal College of Physicians (MRCP) in 1979, and served as a renal registrar at the old 3Ps institute (St Peter’s, St Paul’s and St Philip’s hospitals) from 1979 to 1981.

James was then appointed Medical Research Council research fellow at Northwick Park; in 1985, he earned his Doctor of Medicine (MD) after successful research on “A Spectrum of Immunity in Tuberculosis.”

While working in London he married Vivian Eruchalu in 1981; they had two sons (James and David) and a daughter (Chioma). In 1984 he returned to Nigeria and set up the first dialysis unit in Enugu, pioneering both haemodialysis and peritoneal dialysis services. He also chaired the committee that combated a Lassa fever outbreak in 1989.

His genuine passion for medicine shone through his dedication and commitment. Over forty-two years, he served the profession in Nigeria, Saudi Arabia and the United Kingdom.

In 1989 James moved to Saudi Arabia to work as a consultant nephrologist. In 1995 he was appointed Consultant Physician and Nephrologist at The Royal Free University Hospital and North Middlesex University Hospital in London, a position he held for twenty-one years. He also served as a Tutor and Examiner for the Royal College of Physicians of London for decades.

Research & Publications

A prolific scholar

JK was a prolific research scholar. According to Harzing’s Publish or Perish software (2017), between 1978 and 1999 he had published well over 30 research journal papers which were cited over 700 times by his peers. JK was either the sole author or first named author in over 85% of these publications — a remarkable record that reflects his intellectual rigour and leadership in the field.

Honours & Achievements

Recognition at the highest level

Starting from humble beginnings in a deprived village school in Ogidi, JK eventually made it to the Royal College of Physicians of London. His exceptional contributions to medicine were recognised with numerous prestigious awards:

Fellow of the Royal College of Physicians

One of the most distinguished honours in British medicine, recognising his outstanding contributions to medical practice and scholarship.

MRCP — Highest Score

He passed the Membership of the Royal College of Physicians with the highest score among candidates at the time, a testament to his exceptional clinical knowledge.

Personal Life

A devoted family man

Beyond his professional achievements, Dr. Onwubalili was a devoted family man. He married Dr. (Mrs.) Vivian Onwubalili (née Onyemelukwe), herself a distinguished medical professional. Together, they raised a family that continues to uphold the values of excellence, service, and education that defined his life.

James was delighted when all three of his children entered medical school, qualifying from University College London Hospitals. His sons, Dr. Emeka Onwubalili and David Onwubalili, carry forward his legacy — Emeka as an NHS consultant and David as a communications professional.

Legacy

A life that continues to inspire

Dr. James Kenechukwu Onwubalili died peacefully at home on 4 May 2017 at the age of 70, surrounded by his family. Tributes from colleagues and former trainees emphasise his patience, enthusiasm for teaching and enduring pride in his children.

The James Kenechukwu Onwubalili Foundation aims to formalise and continue a cause James quietly pursued throughout his life — generously supporting the education of less privileged children through financial assistance, a mission that brought him immense satisfaction.

Dr. J.K. Onwubalili’s legacy of excellence, humility, sacrifice and dignity will forever resound in the minds of those who knew him well.
