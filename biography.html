1947 — 2017

Dr. James Kenechukwu Onwubalili

MD FRCP — Senior Consultant Physician and Nephrologist. A life defined by excellence, humility, sacrifice and an unwavering commitment to education.

Early Life and Education

James Kenechukwu Onwubalili (known as “JK”) was born on 24 April 1947 in Ogidi, Anambra State, Nigeria. Tragically, he lost his father, Rev. James Onwubalili, when he was just three years old in 1950. This loss made paying JK’s school fees a significant challenge, as his widowed mother’s limited earnings were insufficient.

After completing his primary education at Ebenezer Primary School in Ogidi, JK was admitted to one of Nigeria’s finest male colleges, Government College Umuahia. There, he excelled through hard work and determination. To complete his secondary education, JK received financial assistance from his uncles, occasional gifts from philanthropists and his mother’s modest savings.

His undergraduate studies at the University of Ibadan were interrupted by the Biafran civil war. During the conflict he joined the Biafran army, rising to the rank of captain, and returned to complete his medical education after the war. James was an outstanding student; he won several prizes and qualified MB BS in 1975 with distinction. As the top student in his class, he was awarded the prestigious Victor Ludorum, collecting medals for medicine, obstetrics, child health and pathology.

Medical Training and Early Career

After his MBBS, JK travelled to the UK for postgraduate studies specialising in nephrology. He trained in renal medicine at some of London’s top universities. His first post was as a renal senior house officer (SHO) at Hammersmith Hospital. He subsequently worked as a renal SHO at Northwick Park Hospital, gained the Membership of the Royal College of Physicians (MRCP) in 1979, and served as a renal registrar at the old 3Ps institute (St Peter’s, St Paul’s and St Philip’s hospitals) from 1979 to 1981. James was then appointed Medical Research Council research fellow at Northwick Park; in 1984, he earned his Doctor of Medicine (MD) after successful research on “A Spectrum of Immunity in Tuberculosis.”

While working in London he married Vivian Eruchalu in 1981; they had two sons (James and David) and a daughter (Chioma). In 1984 he returned to Nigeria and set up the first dialysis unit in Enugu, pioneering both haemodialysis and peritoneal dialysis services and training a new team of dialysis nurses and technicians. He also chaired the committee that combated a Lassa fever outbreak in 1989 and served as director of postgraduate education at the University of Nigeria medical school (1986–1989).

International Career and Contributions to Nephrology

In 1989 James moved to Saudi Arabia to work as a consultant nephrologist, while his wife and children returned to England to continue their education. In 1995 he was appointed Consultant Physician and Nephrologist at The Royal Free University Hospital and North Middlesex University Hospital in London, a position he held for twenty-one years. He collaborated with the Royal London Hospital to establish a new haemodialysis unit and became a strong advocate for better renal-care access for patients from ethnic minorities.

His genuine passion for medicine shone through his dedication and commitment. Over forty-two years, he served the profession in Nigeria, Saudi Arabia and the United Kingdom. He also served as a Tutor and Examiner for the Royal College of Physicians of London for decades, and worked as an associate professor for medical students from St George’s University, Grenada. His colleagues describe him as a gentle, dignified physician who inspired loyalty and affection from patients and staff.

Research and Publications

JK was a prolific research scholar. According to Harzing’s Publish or Perish software (2017), which looks up scholarly citations and calculates several citation and impact metrics, between 1978 and 1999 he had published well over 30 research journal papers which were cited over 700 times by his peers. JK was either the sole author or first named author in over 85% of these publications — a remarkable record that reflects his intellectual rigour and leadership in the field.

Honours and Achievements

Starting from humble beginnings in a deprived village school in Ogidi, JK eventually made it to the Royal College of Physicians of London. His exceptional contributions to medicine were recognised with numerous prestigious awards and honours:

Fellow of the Royal College of Physicians

He was elected a Fellow of the Royal College of Physicians, one of the most distinguished honours in British medicine, recognising his outstanding contributions to medical practice and scholarship.

Gold Medal — Royal College of Physicians of Ireland

He was awarded the Gold Medal by the Royal College of Physicians of Ireland, a rare and prestigious honour bestowed upon physicians who have made exceptional contributions to the advancement of medicine. He passed the MRCP with the highest score among candidates at the time.

Personal Life

Beyond his professional achievements, Dr. Onwubalili was a devoted family man. He married Dr. (Mrs.) Vivian Onwubalili (née Onyemelukwe), herself a distinguished medical professional. Together, they raised a family that continues to uphold the values of excellence, service, and education that defined his life.

James was delighted when all three of his children entered medical school, qualifying from University College London Hospitals. His sons, Dr. Emeka Onwubalili and David Onwubalili, carry forward his legacy — Emeka as an NHS consultant and David as a communications professional. The determination of his widow Vivian and their children have been instrumental to the realisation of the JKO Foundation.

Legacy

Dr. James Kenechukwu Onwubalili died peacefully at home on 4 May 2017 at the age of 70, surrounded by his family. Tributes from colleagues and former trainees emphasise his patience, enthusiasm for teaching and enduring pride in his children.

Without the support of philanthropists during his early education, JK might not have achieved his remarkable success and become a pillar of support for many people and families. The James Kenechukwu Onwubalili Foundation aims to formalise and continue a cause James quietly pursued throughout his life — generously supporting the education of less privileged children through financial assistance, a mission that brought him immense satisfaction.

Dr. J.K. Onwubalili’s legacy of excellence, humility, sacrifice and dignity will forever resound in the minds of those who knew him well.
