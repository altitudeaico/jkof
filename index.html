The Story

From village school at Ogidi to the Royal College

Born in Ogidi, Anambra State, Nigeria, James was only three years old when his father died in 1950. His widowed mother's meagre earnings were insufficient to pay his school fees.

Through the support of uncles, philanthropists, and his mother's savings, James excelled academically. He graduated from the University of Ibadan Medical School with distinction as the best student of his class in 1975, earning the coveted Gold Medal.

His journey continued to the UK for postgraduate studies in nephrology, culminating in a Doctor of Medicine in 1984. That little boy from village school at Ogidi eventually became a Fellow of the Royal College of Physicians of London.

Without the support of philanthropists, JK may never have overcome his financial limitations. The James Kenechukwu Onwubalili Foundation continues his legacy by supporting intelligent, motivated students who face similar challenges.
