Our Story

A Legacy of Excellence

The JK Onwubalili Foundation is a non-profit organisation established in memory of Dr. James Kenechukwu (JK) Onwubalili, who passed away on 4th May 2017 after a remarkable life.

JK’s life was truly remarkable and equally inspiring to those who knew of his humble beginnings. His education began at a small village school in Ogidi, Anambra State, Nigeria. Tragically, he lost his father, Rev. James Onwubalili, when he was just three years old in 1950. This loss made paying JK’s school fees a significant challenge, as his widowed mother’s limited earnings were insufficient.

After completing his primary education at Ebenezer Primary School in Ogidi, JK was admitted to one of Nigeria’s finest male colleges, Government College Umuahia. There, he excelled through hard work and determination. To complete his secondary education, JK received financial assistance from his uncles, occasional gifts from philanthropists and his mother’s modest savings.

With these resources, JK was able to attend the University of Ibadan Medical School, where he graduated in medicine with distinction. As the top student in his class of 1975, he was awarded the prestigious Victor Ludorum.

Starting from humble beginnings in a deprived village school in Ogidi, Nigeria, JK eventually made it to the Royal College of Physicians of London, becoming a Fellow of the College.

After his MBBS, JK travelled to the UK for postgraduate studies specialising in nephrology. He trained in renal medicine at some of London’s top universities. In 1985, he earned his Doctor of Medicine (MD) after successful research on “A Spectrum of Immunity in Tuberculosis.”

JK was a prolific research scholar. According to Harzing’s Publish or Perish software (2017), between 1978 and 1999, he had published well over 30 research journal papers which were cited over 700 times by his peers. JK was either the sole author or first named author in over 85% of these publications.

His genuine passion for medicine shone through his dedication and commitment as a doctor. Over forty years, he served the profession in Nigeria, Saudi Arabia and the UK. His final appointment was as Consultant Physician and Nephrologist at The Royal Free University Hospital and North Middlesex University Hospital, London, for twenty-one years. He also served as a Tutor and Examiner for the Royal College of Physicians of London for decades.

Without the support of philanthropists during his early education, JK might not have achieved his remarkable success and become a pillar of support for many people and families.

The James Kenechukwu Onwubalili Foundation aims to formalise and continue a cause James quietly pursued throughout his life. He generously supported the education of less privileged children through financial assistance, a mission that brought him immense satisfaction.

Our vision is to carry on JK’s legacy, hoping our contributions will make a difference in the lives of other disadvantaged pupils.

Background

About the Foundation

James Kenechukwu Onwubalili Foundation is named after Dr. James Kenechukwu Onwubalili (JKO), a Senior Consultant Physician and Nephrologist who worked for over 30 years at National Health Service (NHS) Teaching Hospitals in London.

While James’ forty-two-year career as a Doctor was predominantly in the United Kingdom, his services and positive contributions extended to Nigeria and Saudi Arabia.

James died on 4th May 2017 at the age of 70 years, following a period of ill health. The determination of his widow (Vivian) and their children have been instrumental to the realisation of the JKO Foundation.

James Kenechukwu Onwubalili Foundation (NIG) was registered with the Corporate Affairs Commission Nigeria in 2023. It is an affiliate of the parent foundation, James Kenechukwu Onwubalili Foundation (UK), which is registered as a Charitable Incorporated Organisation of England and Wales (2026).

The Trustees for the parent and affiliate foundations come from diverse professional backgrounds. They include medical doctors from different specialties (Surgery, Nephrology, Public Health, Haematology, Accident & Emergency, General Practice), a Certified Independent/Non-Executive Director (Financial Sector), University Lecturers, a Headteacher, a Real Estate Consultant, Deputy Managing Director at Society for Family Health and a Lawyer.

Dr. J.K. Onwubalili’s legacy of excellence, humility, sacrifice and dignity will forever resound in the minds of those who knew him well.
