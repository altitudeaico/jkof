Foundation History

How the James Kenechukwu Onwubalili Foundation was established to continue a legacy of educational empowerment.

Established July 2023

Our Story

The James Kenechukwu Onwubalili Foundation was established in July 2023 by the family of Dr. James Kenechukwu Onwubalili to honour his extraordinary legacy and continue his lifelong commitment to educational excellence.

Dr. JK believed passionately that education was the most powerful instrument for changing the world. Throughout his distinguished career as a physician and Fellow of the Royal College of Physicians, he championed the cause of academically gifted students who lacked the financial means to pursue their potential.

Following his passing, his family — led by Dr. (Mrs.) Vivian Onwubalili and their sons Dr. Emeka Onwubalili and David Onwubalili — resolved to establish a foundation that would embody his values and ensure that his vision for educational empowerment would endure for generations to come.

The Foundation was registered as a dual entity: JKOF (UK) as a Charitable Incorporated Organisation in England and Wales, and JKOF (NIG) with registration number 7061941 in Nigeria. This dual structure reflects Dr. JK's own journey — from his roots in Nigeria to his distinguished career in the United Kingdom — and ensures that the Foundation can serve students in both countries.

Since its establishment, the Foundation has awarded over 20 scholarships across 7 partner schools, supporting academically exceptional students who would otherwise be unable to access quality education. Each scholarship recipient carries forward Dr. JK's belief that excellence, when given the opportunity to flourish, has the power to transform not only individual lives but entire communities.

Governance

Board of Trustees — United Kingdom

The UK entity is registered as a Charitable Incorporated Organisation in England and Wales, governed by a dedicated board of trustees.

Dr. (Mrs.) Vivian Onwubalili

Chairperson

Née Onyemelukwe, Dr. Vivian is a distinguished medical professional and the widow of Dr. JK. She leads the Foundation with a deep personal commitment to continuing her husband's legacy of educational empowerment.

Dr. Emeka Onwubalili

Secretary / Treasurer

Dr. JK's son, Emeka is a consultant in the National Health Service. He serves as the bridge between the UK and Nigeria entities, ensuring the Foundation's operations remain aligned with its founding mission.

Dr. Joseph Onwubalili

Trustee

Dr. JK's brother, Joseph brings a deep understanding of the family's values and Dr. JK's vision for educational empowerment to his role as trustee of the Foundation.

David Onwubalili

Trustee

Dr. JK's son, David is a communications professional who brings strategic expertise in outreach, engagement, and storytelling to the Foundation's mission.

Board of Trustees — Nigeria

The Nigeria entity (Registration: 7061941) is governed by a board of distinguished professionals and longtime friends of Dr. JK.

Prof. Philip Njemanze

Chairman

A distinguished neuroscientist and childhood friend of Dr. JK, Professor Njemanze brings decades of academic and research leadership to the Foundation's governance in Nigeria.

Raph Oriaku

Secretary

A banker and philanthropist, Raph brings extensive experience in financial management and a deep commitment to charitable work in Nigeria.

Emeka Onwubalili

Director

Serving on both the UK and Nigeria boards, Emeka ensures continuity and alignment between the Foundation's dual entities and their shared mission.

Ike Odumodu

Trustee

A schoolmate of Dr. JK from Government College Umuahia, Ike brings a personal connection to Dr. JK's educational journey and values.

Emma Obi

Trustee

A school friend of Dr. JK, Emma's longstanding friendship and shared experiences inform his commitment to the Foundation's mission in Nigeria.

Obinna Onyemelukwe

Trustee

Vivian's brother and a business executive, Obinna brings strategic business acumen and a deep family connection to the Foundation's governance.
